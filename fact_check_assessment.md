### LEGAL ANALYSIS - ISSUE A

**Strength of Evidence: STRONG**

**Core Facts Established:**
1. ✓ Multi-year paid relationship between Karuna and PGI Drug Discovery/PsychoGenics (2020-2024)
2. ✓ Henry Jarecki is Chairman and founding investor of PsychoGenics since 1999/2002
3. ✓ Payments exceed $120,000 threshold ($3.845M increase in nine months 2020 alone)
4. ✓ Four consecutive proxy statements claim no related-party transactions >$120K
5. ✓ Agreement never filed as material contract exhibit

**Critical Legal Question:**

Does Jarecki's chairmanship of the **counterparty** (PsychoGenics/PGI) constitute a "related person" under Regulation S-K Item 404, **absent a documented direct relationship** between Jarecki and a Karuna insider (director, executive officer, or 5%+ stockholder)?

**Regulation S-K Item 404 Definition of Related Person:**
- Director or executive officer
- Nominee for director
- **Immediate family member** of the above
- Greater than 5% stockholder
- **Any person who was in any such position at any time since the beginning of the last fiscal year**

**Gap in Investigation:**
The investigation document states: "Nine Karuna directors were cross-referenced against Jarecki's documented network; no connection was found in public records."

**CRITICAL WEAKNESS:** If Jarecki has NO direct connection to any Karuna board member, executive officer, or 5%+ stockholder, then the Item 404 nexus breaks down. The regulation requires a "related person" to have a material interest in the transaction - simply being Chairman of the counterparty entity may not satisfy this requirement.

**Counterargument:** The agreement was with PGI Drug Discovery LLC (Jarecki's company) where Jarecki had direct financial interest as chairman and founding investor. If Jarecki personally benefited from the payments, that could establish materiality.

**Potential SEC Arguments:**
1. Jarecki's chairman/investor role gives him material interest in PsychoGenics revenue
2. The payments flowed to an entity he controls/founded
3. Disclosure obligation exists when counterparty leadership has such concentration of control
4. The consistent omission across four filings suggests knowledge/intent

**Weakness of SEC Arguments:**
1. No textual support in Item 404 for "chairman of counterparty entity" as triggering disclosure
2. Arms-length commercial transaction with legitimate business purpose
3. Karuna legal counsel may have concluded (reasonably or not) that no disclosure was required

### LEGAL ANALYSIS - ISSUE B

**Cannot be assessed** without access to the underlying evidence documents.

**IF the EFTA documents exist and contain what is claimed:**

**Form ADV Part 2A Item 9 Requirements:**
- Disclose material legal and disciplinary events
- Events presumed material for 10 years
- Beyond 10 years: disclose if "so serious that it remains currently material"
- Specific triggers require formal proceedings (Items 9.A, 9.B, 9.C)

**Critical Legal Issues (assuming documents exist):**

1. **10-Year Clock:** July 2010 conduct is 15+ years old. The presumption of materiality has expired. Would need to argue "remains currently material" - high bar.

2. **Formal Proceeding Requirement:** Items 9.A, 9.B, 9.C enumerate specific disciplinary triggers that all require **formal proceedings** (criminal actions, administrative proceedings, SRO proceedings). If no formal proceeding was brought in 2010, the conduct may not fall within the enumerated triggers.

3. **Catch-All Provision:** Form ADV states "If there are legal or disciplinary events that are not listed in Items 9.A, 9.B, or 9.C, but nonetheless is material...you must disclose the event." This could theoretically cover the conduct IF it is material and IF it qualifies as a "disciplinary event."

4. **What Constitutes a "Disciplinary Event"?** Forwarding proprietary trading signals to a third party may constitute:
   - Breach of fiduciary duty
   - Misappropriation of proprietary information
   - Violation of client confidentiality
   - Potential insider trading (if signals constituted material non-public information)
   
   However, without a formal investigation or proceeding, classifying this as a "disciplinary event" is legally questionable.
