## Subject Biography

**Louella E. Rabuyo** is a Filipino-American domestic worker whose documented employment history with Jeffrey Epstein begins, per Epstein's own personnel/payroll records, on **November 16, 2004**, when she was hired as a housekeeper at the Palm Beach residence at 358 El Brillo Way. She was interviewed by Ghislaine Maxwell at the Palm Beach residence and, upon hiring, signed a confidentiality agreement — a condition imposed uniformly on Epstein household staff. Her documented duties included tidying, making beds, and laundry. She resided in the staff house on property and had direct access to Epstein for administrative matters including personal time-off approvals. Her compensation began at a figure variously reported between **$32,000 and $35,000 annually**, climbed to **$42,000 by 2007** (with some notes extending that figure to 2007–2008), and during its peak years carried bonuses of **$3,000–$5,000**, **health insurance**, **life insurance**, **dental coverage**, and **Simple IRA retirement contributions**, bringing total compensation to **$50,520–$63,877 annually**. Compensation then **declined** in the post-2008-prosecution period to **$37,800 (2009–2010)** by one account and **$46,758 by 2010** by another. She was deposed on **October 20, 2009** by plaintiffs' attorney **Bradley Edwards** in the civil proceedings; Epstein paid for her legal representation alongside parallel coverage of counsel for **Sarah Kellen**, **Ghislaine Maxwell**, and other household staff and associates. In her testimony she acknowledged the volume of young female visitors but disclaimed knowledge of what occurred behind bedroom doors; she encountered Maxwell only three times during her 2004–2005 employment window. Administrative emails document her involvement in routine staff coordination and scheduling through at least 2016. Unresolved: one enrichment note claims she met Maxwell at **Palm Beach Atlantic College** in **late February/early March 2001** and began working for her the following weekend — a chronology approximately **3.5 years earlier** than the November 2004 hire date that dominates the other notes. A separate claim that she purchased a **foreclosed Bexar County (San Antonio) home ~August 2001** — "approximately 3 months after Giuffre and Epstein were in San Antonio in May 2001" — is **not documented** in the chunks reviewed, though FBI San Antonio/Austin witness work in **November 2006** is confirmed and Bexar County addresses do surface in the investigation profiles.

**Crispin Pesasico** appears in the Epstein document collection **only once**, via a background check that surfaces **census data** (median income **$61,765**, median home value **$194,737**). No investigative connection to Epstein operations is established. He is separately identified as the respondent-party in Florida Domestic Relations case **50-2008-DR-006289-XXXX-MB** — *RABUYO, LOUELLA E V PESASICO, CRISPIN* — filed in Palm Beach County in **2008**, characterized as a personal family-law dispute (likely divorce). Keyword searches for "Pesasico & Rabuyo" in Epstein files returned no co-occurrence results. Pesasico also appears in **Rule 6(e) confidential grand jury documents** with household information redacted.

**Robert Foos** is documented as a recipient of regular consultant-style payments from **TerraMar (Maxwell)**. The only precisely cited Foos transaction is a **$2,033.69 payment from the Terramar Project on June 16, 2014**. No document links Foos to Pleasant Tree Trust, to Florence McNiff, to Rabuyo's property acquisitions, or to any Boynton Beach condo purchase.

**Florence McNiff** is named in the source question as the alleged original owner / trustee of "Pleasant Tree Trust" and the seller of the Boynton Beach condo that Rabuyo reportedly purchased in 2015. The notes confirm only that a **Florence McNiff sold a property on 6/30–7/1/2015** and that a **Boynton Beach condo sold on 7/23/2015 for $130,000 with a redacted buyer**. "Pleasant Tree Trust" is **not mentioned** in any Epstein document reviewed.
