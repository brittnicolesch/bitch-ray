# Elite Dark Money & Super PACs: The Regulatory Capture

**Overview:** The technological architecture of the police state requires political protection to survive. This document tracks the flow of "Dark Money" from the Tech Oligarchy—specifically the "PayPal Mafia" and the Dialog network—into federal elections, ensuring that Congress remains compliant with the privatization of intelligence and surveillance.

## 1. The 2022 Midterm Surge: Funding the "New Right"
During the 2022 election cycle, Peter Thiel shifted from a background player to a massive, overt financier of ideological allies, injecting unprecedented capital to install sympathetic lawmakers.

*   **The War Chest:** Thiel donated approximately $35 million across 16 federal races during the 2022 cycle, focusing heavily on candidates aligned with his "New Right" and nationalist-populist vision [[1]](https://ballotpedia.org/).
*   **JD Vance & Blake Masters:** Thiel single-handedly backed his former employees and ideological proteges. He donated $15 million to a super PAC supporting JD Vance’s successful U.S. Senate campaign in Ohio, and another $20 million to the "Saving Arizona" super PAC backing Blake Masters [[2]](https://www.businessinsider.com/).

## 2. The 2024 "Hiatus" Strategy
Following the 2022 midterms, Thiel executed a highly publicized withdrawal from political funding.

*   **Public Disillusionment:** Thiel publicly stated to *The Atlantic* and other outlets that he was disillusioned with the political process and intended to sit out the 2024 presidential election cycle entirely, claiming he was locking himself into that decision [[3]](https://www.theguardian.com/).
*   **Strategic Ambiguity:** Despite intense pressure from his own network (including JD Vance) to back Donald Trump, Thiel maintained this public stance, effectively withholding capital to leverage policy concessions or simply avoiding the chaos of the presidential race [[4]](https://www.businessinsider.com/).

## 3. The 2025 Return: Locking Down the House
Thiel’s political "hiatus" was short-lived. By early 2025, he returned to funding, but with a highly targeted, structural approach aimed at the legislature rather than the executive.

*   **"Grow the Majority":** In February 2025, Thiel donated $852,200 to House Speaker Mike Johnson’s joint fundraising committee, "Grow the Majority" [[5]](https://www.motherjones.com/).
*   **Distributed Influence:** This massive cash injection was subsequently distributed to the Republican National Committee, various state parties, and nearly 30 GOP House members, effectively purchasing broad congressional loyalty and ensuring the House of Representatives remained friendly to his regulatory interests [[6]](https://www.businessinsider.com/).
