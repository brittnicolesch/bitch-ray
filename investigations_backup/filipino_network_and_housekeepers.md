# The Filipino Operations Network

The Epstein enterprise relied heavily on a structured underbelly of Filipino employees who managed the intimate, daily operations across all his primary properties and vessels. Many of these individuals were required to sign strict confidentiality agreements.

2. **Jun and Emily Panlaqui:** Another Filipino couple actively recruited for a position in Palm Beach by Barbara Goldman of **Regal Domestics Inc.**

6. **Randy Amparo:** Landscaper and boat captain for Little St. James island since September 2013.
7. **Reyna Amparo & Basillia Morales-Mercado:** Head housekeepers.
8. **Myla Trestiza:** Chief Stewardess managing staff on Epstein's yacht.
9. **Merwin Dela Cruz:** Chief Engineer on the yacht. His American Express flight records show frequent bookings on **Philippine Airlines** to Manila.
10. **Phil Baker:** Yacht crew member and associate.
11. **Arnie Mercado:** A 50-year-old Filipino caregiver hired to care for someone named Warren.
12. **"Crispin":** Identified in the research logs as a potential relative doing military/guard training in the Philippines, raising questions about whether the recruitment pipeline functioned strictly for domestic labor, or if it intersected with intelligence or trafficking pipelines.

## Operational Deployment
- **New York Residence:** The Fontanillas managed the primary Hub.
- **Island Operations (LSJ):** The Amparos and others handled property management and transit.
- **Maritime Operations:** Trestiza, Dela Cruz, and Baker managed the yacht infrastructure, coordinated by **Wilson Yacht Management**.
- **Travel Logistics:** Operations utilized **Mango Tours** (a Philippine-based travel agency) and frequent **Philippine Airlines** bookings for staff transit.

## The 2010 SEO & Reputation Scrubbing Operation
A massive, organized digital cover-up was managed directly out of the Philippines. Following his 2008 conviction, Epstein and Al Seckel (husband of Ghislaine Maxwell's sister) hired a Philippine-based team for online reputation management. 
- **The Operation:** The team built links to "pseudo sites," edited Wikipedia entries, and flooded Google search results to bury terms like "jail," "pedophile," and "sex offender," replacing them with philanthropy and science support.
- **Cost:** Epstein paid roughly $10,000–$20,000 per month for this operation.
- **Communications:** In late 2010, Seckel reported that "Our group in the Philippines is building links... the old sites will just get moved out of the way. Poof." This sparked official scrutiny in Manila, with the Cybercrime Investigation and Coordinating Centre (CICC) reviewing the network.
