# THE SHADOWS OF ZORRO RANCH: Polo, Passports, and the Tale of Two Wendys

The desert hides its secrets well. But inside the 10,000-acre perimeter of Jeffrey Epstein’s Zorro Ranch in Stanley, New Mexico, the darkest secrets weren’t buried in the sand. They were standing in the stables.

For decades, the billionaire class has utilized the elite equestrian world—racehorses, dressage, and the high-society polo scene—as the ultimate laundering vehicle. It is an insular economy where tens of millions of dollars change hands in private sales, where the valuation of an animal is entirely subjective, and where massive estates are heavily tax-subsidized as "agricultural operations" (Cassidy 42). It is an open secret that many of the families who own the world's most prestigious stables have gone on the record admitting they do not actually care about the horses (Smith).

The horses are simply the vehicle.

For Leslie Wexner and Jeffrey Epstein, the equestrian scene served multiple, sinister purposes: it was an untraceable way to move money, and more importantly, it provided frictionless access to the most exclusive social circles in the world. Wexner built a massive, 340-acre equestrian estate in New Albany, Ohio, and maintained a heavy presence in Wellington, Florida—the winter equestrian capital of the world (Wexner Equestrian Holdings). The polo fields and VIP paddocks were fertile hunting grounds to meet new women, traffic victims across state lines under the guise of "equestrian staff," and recruit new power brokers into their web (Doe v. Epstein).

At the center of this social engineering was Ghislaine Maxwell. She didn't just stumble into the horse world; she was born into its aristocratic center. Maxwell’s royal connection to Sarah Ferguson ("Fergie") wasn't just a coincidence of wealth—it was an equestrian blood-tie. Fergie’s father, Major Ronald Ferguson, was the renowned polo manager for Prince Charles and the deputy chairman of the Guards Polo Club (Ferguson). 

To cement this access, Ghislaine Maxwell operated out of a townhouse at **44 Kinnerton Street** in Belgravia, London. This specific address sat just a few hundred meters from the **Royal Mews** at Buckingham Palace—the operational equestrian headquarters for the British Royal Family (Kinnerton Property Records). This proximity provided a highly discreet, secure environment for royals like Prince Andrew to move between official equestrian duties and Maxwell's residence. Through Fergie—who explicitly thanked Epstein in leaked emails for his financial generosity in clearing her massive personal debts—Ghislaine possessed the ultimate VIP pass to the royal polo scene, leveraging those connections to legitimize Epstein among the British aristocracy (Ferguson, EFTA Emails).

But at Zorro Ranch, the horses provided cover for something far more terrifying than money laundering.

### The Trojan Horse: Wendy Nun, Crestview, and the Illegal Science
In 2005, Wendy Nun (née Chapot) assumed the role of Equestrian Director at Zorro Ranch. She wasn’t just a trainer; she was equestrian royalty. The daughter of Olympic legends Frank and Mary Chapot, she brought an impenetrable veneer of prestige to Epstein’s desert compound (Chapot).

She also brought a direct pipeline to cutting-edge genetic engineering.

The Chapot family operated Chado Farm in New Jersey, where they bred *Gem Twist*, one of the most celebrated show jumpers in history. Because Gem Twist was a gelding, he could not reproduce naturally. To preserve his bloodline, the Chapot family contracted with ViaGen—the commercial standard-bearer for animal cloning—to cryopreserve his tissue and clone him using Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer (SCNT) (ViaGen).

In the elite equestrian world, the ethics of this science were fiercely debated and heavily restricted. The Jockey Club, which governs elite Thoroughbred racehorses, explicitly outlaws cloning (The Jockey Club). It is illegal to race a cloned horse in any official capacity. Yet, exploiting regulatory loopholes in show jumping and polo, the billionaire network pushed forward anyway. The cloned horses were never intended for the race track; they were used as breeding stock and polo mounts, operating on the absolute fringes of biological ethics where billions of dollars override regulations. 

This cloning loophole birthed a massive commercial enterprise. In 2009, Texas oilman Alan Meeker, Argentine businessman Ernesto Gutierrez, and polo legend Adolfo Cambiaso founded Crestview Genetics to clone polo ponies using ViaGen's technology (Crestview Genetics). Meeker actively played on the Dubai Polo Team, owned by Emirati billionaire Ali Albwardy, providing a direct social and logistical bridge to Gulf royalty (Albwardy Investments). 

For Jeffrey Epstein, this was the perfect Trojan Horse. 

### The Saudi DNA Trades and the Transhumanist Vision
Epstein was dangerously obsessed with transhumanism. During this exact time period, he was fanatically collecting human genetic material. Internal records show him dispatching 30+ DNA kits to Gulf VIPs (including Sultan Bin Sulayem of Dubai) through his JFK pipeline via Emirates Airlines (Epstein, "Re: Swabs"). He funded Harvard geneticist George Church ($2 million) and employed concierge doctors to sequence his own genome and store his stem cells in liquid nitrogen, openly boasting of his desire to "seed the human race" (Church).

This ambition extended directly to the Saudi Royal Court. Epstein aggressively courted Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS) during the rise of "Vision 2030"—MBS's plan for a tech-utopian mega-city called NEOM. Epstein acted as a back-channel broker, communicating frequently with **Raafat Al-Sabbagh**, a senior adviser to the Saudi Royal Court who visited Epstein's Manhattan townhouse (Al-Sabbagh Emails).

The bridge between them was transhumanism. Epstein was a primary funder of Ben Goertzel and **Hanson Robotics**—the very company that built *"Sophia the Robot"* (Hanson Robotics Grants). In 2017, MBS famously granted Sophia Saudi citizenship as a massive PR stunt for Vision 2030. Epstein wasn't just trafficking women; he was trading genetic data, transhumanist AI, and eugenics infrastructure for sovereign wealth access.

By employing Wendy Nun to run his stables, Epstein had a flawless, legitimate justification to purchase and install IVF machines, cryopreservation tanks, and embryology tools at Zorro Ranch. If federal regulators saw liquid nitrogen arriving in the desert, they were told it was for the elite horses. 

### The Corporate Escape Route
In July 2011, the operation pivoted. Wendy Nun sent a final email offering to "sell the entire stock" of horses and vanished from the Zorro Ranch footprint (Nun, "Horse Sale").

Her exit was clean, quiet, and remarkably lucrative. Scrubbing her resume of the desert compound, Nun transitioned immediately into white-collar corporate America. Today, she sits as a Managing Director of Ethics & Compliance at KPMG (Nunn, LinkedIn).

The hypocrisy is staggering. KPMG is the very accounting firm that audited Silicon Valley Bank (which shared Ghislaine Maxwell's New Hampshire shell-company address). KPMG was the training ground for Leon Black, the Apollo Global billionaire who paid Epstein $158 million for "tax advice" (Black). The woman who oversaw the stables at a human trafficking hub now dictates corporate ethics for the firm shielding Epstein’s billionaire backers.

### The Exile of Wendy Martinez
Her replacement, however, would not be afforded a corporate parachute.

Enter Wendy Martinez. Hired in August 2011 to take over the equestrian operations alongside ranch manager Manolito Royal ("Mon"), Martinez was a working-class manager raising her son, **AJ Martinez**, on the property. While Martinez took over the stables, she did not inherit the Olympic prestige or the genetic cover story afforded to Nun.

Instead, she and Royal found themselves at the center of a deteriorating professional relationship. Manolito Royal, a 17-year veteran of the ranch, also lived on-site with his teenage son, **Ean Royal**.

By early 2012, Martinez and Royal realized they were being boxed out of their own operation. The administration began keeping them completely in the dark regarding the physical facilities. Knowing that secrets were being kept from them, Martinez and Royal submitted a formal "proposal" to Epstein, essentially begging to be let in and given more responsibility over the ranch's operations. Epstein flatly denied them.

Following the denial, the retaliation was swift. In a desperate and haunting email sent to Epstein's enforcer Rich Kahn on February 17, 2012, Martinez documented her confusion over the bizarre mistreatment of the animals, specifically questioning why they were banned from bringing the horses inside the actual stalls, forcing them to eat in sand runs that could cause fatal colic.

> *"...I will-not feed horses in a run with sand. Sand, when ingested can clog/block a horses intestine - causing them to colic and sometimes this leads to death. It is a very horrible death... Now the horses can't come in the inside stalls. What am I to do? ... Now this morning Steve said [Redacted] called him last night and said that JE said 'no nags in the stables'. Even if there is a snow storm they are not allowed inside. First, I can't believe that JE would call his horses 'nags' as they have provided so much enjoyment to our guests... I can not believe that my credibility and use of the stables is being taken away by people that don't like or know anything about horses... Why are Mano and myself not allowed to use the stables for our horses? - we live here."* (Martinez, "Zorro Ranch Horses/stables")

This "no horses in stalls" policy was not merely a management whim. It provided a direct overlap with the era when **Virginia Giuffre** and other victims were documented at the ranch. Giuffre explicitly stated she lived and stayed at Zorro Ranch between 2000 and 2002, and Epstein himself possessed photos of her on horseback at the property. Wendy Martinez’s job was literally to manage the horses these guests rode, putting her in the direct line of sight of the "models" arriving at the compound.

Instead of answers, the ranch went into a state of severe paranoia. Security cameras were installed to monitor Martinez and Royal. They were physically locked out of the stables and denied access to computers. Epstein's Brooklyn-based real estate and financial fixers were brought in to tighten the perimeter.

Then, the tipping point arrived. In June 2012, an urgent tip reached the Albuquerque FBI field office alleging that "girls were being kept in stalls" at Zorro Ranch (Albuquerque FBI Tip).

The network’s response was immediate and panicked. Realizing the danger of the escalating lockdown, Wendy Martinez and Manolito Royal attempted to flee the property in the middle of the night. Their escape triggered a massive containment response. The ranch was thrown into absolute lockdown. Epstein’s enforcer, Rich Kahn, ordered the immediate seizure of their personal property. Their keys, credit cards, and cell phones were confiscated. Even their personal vehicles and tools were inventoried and secured.

In the records of June 19, 2012, the silence is deafening. Manolito and Wendy’s resignation was finalized under extreme duress. Their home was emptied. Their dog, Groosky, was left in a mudroom. And most chillingly, Wendy Martinez, AJ Martinez, Manolito Royal, and Ean Royal were purged from the ranch footprint.

While the corporate insiders like Wendy Nun moved on to KPMG and Apollo, the working-class insiders who saw the "girls in stalls" were silenced.

### The Long Cleanup
The disappearance of the families in 2012 was not the end of the Zorro Ranch cleanup.

In 2018, a surgical burglary at the home of pilot Larry Visoski resulted in the theft of 33 guns and specific photo evidence. In June 2020, just prior to Ghislaine Maxwell's arrest, security logs documented a massive "evidence scrub" at Zorro Ranch, specifically targeting the large utility garages designated as **Structures R09 and R16**—the exact outbuildings capable of holding heavy cryogenic and IVF equipment.

By 2026, the silence was finally broken. During depositions for the New Mexico Truth Commission, **Manolito Royal invoked his Fifth Amendment right** against self-incrimination, refusing to identify the high-profile male "VIP" visitors he had seen at the ranch over his 17-year tenure.

His son, **Ean Royal**, has since emerged as a key whistleblower. Ean has publicly identified suspicious "concrete slabs" and areas of frequent dirt movement near the stables and the main mansion, urging investigators to use ground-penetrating radar to search for potential burial sites.

Today, the physical evidence of what Wendy Martinez and the Royal family saw is being systematically erased. But the testimony of the children who grew up on the ranch—Ean and AJ—may finally shed light on the girls who were kept in the stalls where the horses were no longer allowed.
