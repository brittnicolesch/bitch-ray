## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This investigation identifies a potential layered offshore structure connecting a failed biotech company (Goldfinch Bio) to an established offshore financial network associated with Henry Jarecki through a Toronto intermediary hub. The structure exhibits characteristics consistent with royalty payment concealment and beneficial ownership obfuscation.

**KEY FINDINGS:**

1. **LIGHTHOUSE GENOMICS → HAWTHORNE ACQUISITION (2023):** Lighthouse Genomics Inc. (BC, Canada) transferred trademark rights to Hawthorne Canada Limited on June 29, 2023, using TMF Canada Inc. as registered agent at Suite 820, 330 Bay Street, Toronto.

2. **TORONTO HUB PATTERN:** Multiple entities share Suite 820, 330 Bay Street address with common directors (Albert J. Messina, Adam Hanscom, Christopher J. Hagedorn), including: Hawthorne Canada Limited, Gavita Canada Inc., Agrolux Lighting Holding Inc., and AeroGrow International Inc.

3. **GOLDFINCH BIO ABC LIQUIDATION (2023):** Goldfinch Bio assigned all assets to GFB (ABC), LLC (Delaware) on Jan 6, 2023; ABC proceeding filed Jan 17, 2023 (DE Court of Chancery Case 2023-0045); licensed TRPC4/5 assets to Karuna Therapeutics for $15M upfront + $520M milestones on Jan 31, 2023.

4. **JARECKI OFFSHORE INFRASTRUCTURE:** Documented BVI/USVI network controlled by Henry Jarecki through Falconwood Corporation, including: Life Entitlements Corporation (insurance vehicle, 565 5th Ave NYC), Audubon Holdings Ltd (BVI, Norman Island ownership), USVI exempt captive insurance structures.

5. **CRITICAL MISSING LINK:** No documented connection found between Goldfinch Bio/GFB (ABC) LLC and Jarecki network, Toronto hub, or Lighthouse Genomics IN PROJECT FILES.

**INVESTIGATION STATUS:** OPEN - Evidence supports structural capability for offshore royalty routing but lacks direct transactional proof.

## LEGAL AND ETHICAL NOTES

### Jurisdiction and Authority

**Delaware ABC Proceeding:**
- Court has equitable jurisdiction over estate assets
- Creditors have standing to object to distributions
- Court can compel disclosure of beneficial interests
- Fraudulent transfer claims possible if offshore routing proven

**SEC Disclosure Requirements:**
- Karuna's 10-K must disclose material agreements
- Related party transactions require disclosure
- Undisclosed beneficial ownership may violate Reg S-K
- Bristol-Myers Squibb (acquirer) has continuing obligations

**Potential Violations (IF connections proven):**

1. **Securities Fraud (10b-5):**
   - IF GFB (ABC) concealed beneficial ownership
   - IF Karuna disclosed misleading counterparty information
   - IF royalty routing was material and undisclosed

2. **Money Laundering (18 USC §1956):**
   - IF structured to conceal source/ownership of funds
   - IF offshore routing designed to evade reporting
   - Requires proof of predicate offense + intent

3. **Fraudulent Transfer (Delaware Law):**
   - IF ABC estate transferred assets to insiders
   - IF creditors were harmed by offshore routing
   - 4-year lookback period from transfer date

**What This Analysis Can Prove:**
- Entity formation dates and registered agents

**What This Analysis CANNOT Prove Without Additional Records:**

**Standard of Proof Requirements:**
