# LOUELLA RABUYO, ROBERT FOOS, AND THE TERRAMAR MONEY MACHINE

# How Epstein's Network Paid Its Insiders Through a Charity Shell

*An Investigative Report — Updated April 2026*


When Jeffrey Epstein's name re-entered the news cycle in 2019, the focus was — rightly — on the victims. But the network that enabled and protected his crimes for decades extended far beyond the names that made headlines. Among the most revealing threads now emerging from the Epstein Files is a financial pipeline: Epstein money flowing to Maxwell, Maxwell money flowing through TerraMar, and TerraMar money landing in the accounts of people on the inside of the operation — including, the evidence suggests, Epstein's longtime housekeeper and the nonprofit's own Director of Development.

This article presents, for the first time, the complete documented chain connecting these payments — from Epstein's JPMorgan accounts, through Ghislaine Maxwell's personal savings, through the TerraMar Project at UBS, to a Massachusetts shell corporation owned entirely by Robert Walter Foos — and, on the same August day in 2015, to the purchase of a $130,000 cash condominium now occupied by Louella Rabuyo.

## Louella Rabuyo: More Than a Housekeeper

Louella Rabuyo joined Jeffrey Epstein's household in November 2004, hired by Ghislaine Maxwell following an interview at Epstein's Palm Beach mansion. Over fifteen years, her role grew from primary housekeeper to scheduler and logistics coordinator. Court documents confirm she organized dinners with Governor Bill Richardson (EFTA02042721), was copied on Epstein's personal schedule (EFTA02242649), supervised the junior housekeeping staff who cleaned the spaces where abuse occurred, and was asked by Richard Kahn — Epstein's top in-house accountant — to take over senior household operations in 2018 as other long-term staff departed.

Attorney Bradley Edwards, representing Epstein's victims, named her a co-conspirator in civil litigation. Her legal representation was funded by Epstein — part of what Edwards called a systematic effort to make it "nearly impossible to take a deposition of someone that would have helpful information." She gave a deposition in 2009, testified she saw nothing inappropriate, and acknowledged she learned about the abuse allegations "from the news" — despite working in Epstein's home for over a decade. She also acknowledged seeing photographs of topless young women in his closet, and confirmed that Ghislaine Maxwell called her the morning of a police search warrant to delay her arrival until the afternoon.

She was named in Epstein's trust. She received payments after his death. And on August 3, 2015 — the same day a $130,000 wire was sent from Ghislaine Maxwell to the TerraMar Project at UBS — a deed was recorded showing a $130,000 cash purchase of Bent Tree Villas West, Unit 1-101-A, Florida, from Florence McNiff to Louella Rabuyo. A prior email from Louella to Epstein (EFTA02502470, April 24, 2015) thanks him for providing her a "roof over my head" and cites a June 15 closing date. Louella lives in that home today.

## R&C Ventures: The Mystery Is Solved

For months, investigators tracking the TerraMar wire records had flagged a recurring entity: "R&C Ventures, Inc.," receiving bimonthly payments of $3,532.52 from TerraMar's UBS account starting October 1, 2015. The payments continued at minimum through March 2017. The identity of R&C Ventures had been an open question — the Alabama and New York entities bearing similar names had been noted but not confirmed as the recipient.

That question now has a definitive answer.

Massachusetts Secretary of State records (File \#001168658) show that R&C Ventures, Inc. was incorporated as a domestic profit corporation on April 10, 2015 — exactly four months before TerraMar began wiring it money. The registered agent is Robert W. Foos. The president, secretary, treasurer, and director are all Robert Walter Foos. The sole address on file — for the corporation, for its registered agent, and for its president — is 299 Sam Turner Road, East Falmouth, MA 02536. A second principal, Caitlin Quinn Foos, holds the director and vice president roles.

Robert Foos is the same individual who served as Director of Development and Chief of Staff for the TerraMar Project — confirmed by InfluenceWatch and his own Medium profile, where he describes himself as "currently the Director of Development for The TerraMar Project and a former U.S. Coast Guard Officer."

In other words: the person running TerraMar's day-to-day operations created a private corporation four months before his employer began paying that corporation on a bimonthly schedule. He served simultaneously as TerraMar's operational director and as the sole owner, president, and registered agent of the company receiving TerraMar's money. Every dollar wired to R&C Ventures, Inc. was a dollar that went to Robert Foos's home-based corporation — paid by a charity he himself ran, using money that originated with Ghislaine Maxwell, who received it from Jeffrey Epstein.

## Harry Beller: The Man Who Processed It All

Standing behind the entire financial architecture is a figure who has rarely made headlines but whose name appears throughout the Epstein paper trail: Harry Beller, Epstein's personal accountant for 22 years.

Beller worked under Richard Kahn at HBRK, an Epstein-owned entity that civil lawsuits allege was used to facilitate the trafficking operation. He held signatory authority over Epstein's JPMorgan accounts — the accounts from which Epstein paid Maxwell at least $25 million, including a single $19 million transfer documented in the Senate Finance Committee's November 2025 memorandum. His large cash withdrawals from those accounts triggered at least four JPMorgan Suspicious Activity Reports. One of those withdrawals — approximately $100,000 from Hyperion Air, Epstein's aviation entity — was noted in a 2012 JPMorgan internal email.

Beller's name appears on incorporation papers across Epstein's corporate web, on Maxwell's tax documents, and on legal filings spanning from 2013 through 2018. He, Leon Black, John Hannan, and Marc Rowan all worked together at Drexel Burnham Lambert in the 1980s. Black later paid Epstein approximately $50 million in "advisory fees." In June 2025, Beller and his wife Naomi created the HN Beller Legacy Trust — reporting approximately $9.8 billion in assets — with Leon Black as settlor and Black's associates as co-trustees. The trust's prior trustee, Barry Cohen, has resigned without a named successor, leaving governance of a nearly $10 billion estate in question.

Senator Ron Wyden's Finance Committee memorandum is unambiguous: Beller was "an integral part of Epstein's financial operation" and his "potential links to sex trafficking crimes merit further investigation." He has not been charged with any crime. Through his attorney Jonathan Sack, he has declined to comment.

Whether Beller specifically processed the individual wire from Maxwell's account to TerraMar on August 3, 2015 — the wire that matched Louella's closing amount — is not yet established. But as the accountant managing Epstein's accounts with signatory authority during this period, his knowledge of the financial architecture that made these payments possible is a question that Congress and the DOJ have not yet answered.

## The Pattern

- Epstein generated money — through financial manipulation, client payments, and other sources managed by Beller and Kahn.

- Maxwell received that money — at least $25 million documented.

- Maxwell capitalized TerraMar from her personal accounts — creating distance between the source and the ultimate recipients.

- Florence McNiff, the condo seller, is deceased — making it impossible to ask her directly who provided the purchase funds. The closing disbursement records at Keyes/First American would answer this question.

The TerraMar Project reported owing $560,650 to Ghislaine Maxwell on its final IRS return, had $10,252 in cash, and spent nothing on program services in 2018. As one maritime executive who sought TerraMar funding put it: "My impression was that TerraMar as a whole was pretty hollow." It was not hollow — it was a distribution mechanism.
