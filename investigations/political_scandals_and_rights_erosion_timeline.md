# Political Scandals & Rights Erosion Timeline (1996 - 2026)

This ledger tracks the systematic alteration and restriction of civil liberties alongside the expansion of state and corporate power over the last three decades. The primary focus is the United States, but it notes how U.S. policies established global precedents for digital surveillance and speech regulation.

### The 1990s: Precedent and Polarization
*   **1996:** The Telecommunications Act of 1996 is passed in the U.S., triggering massive corporate consolidation of media ownership, laying the groundwork for oligarchic control over public narrative [[1]](https://www.fcc.gov/telecommunications-act-1996).
*   **1998-1999:** The impeachment of Bill Clinton dramatically intensifies American political polarization, shifting public and media focus from structural policy to personal scandal and culture war theatrics.

### The 2000s: The Security State Expansion
*   **2001 (The PATRIOT Act):** Following the 9/11 attacks, Congress swiftly passes the USA PATRIOT Act. It fundamentally erodes Fourth Amendment protections by expanding warrantless wiretapping, "sneak and peek" warrants, and the mass collection of electronic communications [[2]](https://www.aclu.org/issues/national-security/privacy-and-surveillance/surveillance-under-patriot-act). This sets a global precedent for Western intelligence sharing (Five Eyes).
*   **2005-2006 (Warrantless Wiretapping):** Major scandals break regarding the NSA’s warrantless wiretapping of American citizens. Despite public outcry, the Patriot Act is repeatedly reauthorized, normalizing domestic surveillance [[3]](https://www.eff.org/nsa-spying).
*   **2008 (FISA Amendments Act):** Congress passes the FISA Amendments Act, retroactively granting immunity to U.S. telecom companies that participated in the NSA’s warrantless surveillance programs, cementing the fusion of corporate data and state intelligence.

### The 2010s: Dark Money and Whistleblowers
*   **2010 (*Citizens United*):** The Supreme Court's landmark *Citizens United v. FEC* ruling invalidates campaign finance restrictions, allowing unlimited corporate and "Dark Money" spending via Super PACs. This officially legalizes the oligarchic capture of the U.S. legislature [[4]](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/citizens-united-explained).
*   **2013 (*Shelby County*):** The Supreme Court strikes down a key provision of the Voting Rights Act of 1965 (*Shelby County v. Holder*), leading to immediate rollbacks of minority voting protections across multiple states.
*   **2013 (Snowden & NSA Leaks):** Edward Snowden leaks classified documents revealing the PRISM program. The leaks confirm the absolute erosion of digital privacy, proving the U.S. state has backdoor access to the global servers of the Tech Oligarchy, affecting citizens worldwide [[5]](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/jun/06/us-tech-giants-nsa-data).
*   **2018 (Cambridge Analytica):** The scandal reveals how political operatives weaponized Facebook's social graph to deploy algorithmic psychological warfare ("dark nudging") on the American electorate (2016) and the UK (Brexit) [[6]](https://www.ox.ac.uk/research/computational-propaganda).

### The 2020s: Emergency Powers & The Police State
*   **2020-2021 (Pandemic Powers):** Executive branches globally (including the U.S.) utilize pandemic emergency powers to dramatically expand tracking, censorship, and movement restrictions. The economic response triggers the largest upward wealth transfer in modern history.
*   **2022 (Overturning of *Roe v. Wade*):** The U.S. Supreme Court overturns federal abortion protections (*Dobbs v. Jackson*). This immediately triggers civil liberties crises regarding the prosecution of digital communications and location data (e.g., SafeGraph data being used to track clinic visits) [[7]](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2022/06/security-and-privacy-tips-people-seeking-abortion).
*   **2023-2026 (The Privatized Public Square):** As tech billionaires seize control of primary global communication hubs (Twitter/X) and fund the legislature through Dark Money, the state passes bills aimed at restricting internet access (e.g., the RESTRICT Act frameworks), finalizing the architecture of the Technofeudalist Police State.
